Hiking In Algonquin

Interpretive Day Trails for All Levels

The Highway 60 Corridor that cuts through the southern section of Algonquin Provincial Park boasts 14 different interpretive day use hiking trails. Each trail showcases unique landscapes, ecology, wildlife, and history. These unique features are illustrated in companion trail guides which are read in conjunction with interpretive trail markers on each trail. Each of these trails along the corridor are outlined here, with trail name, location along the highway corridor starting from the Parkâ€™s West Gate and moving towards the East Gate, length of trail, estimated duration to hike and challenge level.

The 14 interpretive day trails available in the park are varied in difficulty and take the intrepid hiker on a journey through the parks diverse lanscapes, topography and history.

Algonquin Provincial Park is located in central Ontario and primary access is via Highway 60.

Travel time from the East Gate of the Park to the West Gate, is approximately 41 minutes. Each day trail is located at a noted kilometer marker (See trail mapping for exact lcations).
